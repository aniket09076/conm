Unit 2: Solving Equations

This section focuses on key techniques for solving both non-linear and linear equations, exploring both direct and iterative approaches.

Key Concepts:

Solutions of Non-Linear Equations:

  • Types of Non-Linear Equations: These involve variables with powers, roots, or complex relationships.
  • Polynomial Equations: Equations with terms that involve powers of variables.

Methods to Solve Non-Linear Equations:

  • Direct Methods: These find the solution in one step.
  • Iterative Methods: These methods start with an initial guess and refine it over multiple steps.

Common Iterative Methods:

  • Bisection Method: Divides the interval in half to find the solution.
  • False Position Method: Uses a straight line to improve the estimate.
  • Secant Method: Uses two initial guesses to approximate the solution.
  • Newton-Raphson Method: Uses calculus to refine the estimate based on the current guess.
  • Birge-Vieta Method: Solves polynomial equations efficiently.

Convergence of Iterative Methods:

  • Bisection Method: Slow but reliable convergence.
  • False Position Method: Improvement over bisection but slower in some cases.
  • Newton-Raphson Method: Faster convergence, requires a good initial guess.
  • Secant Method: Similar to Newton-Raphson but doesn't require the function's derivative.

Solutions of Linear Equations:

  • Linear Equations: Equations where variables have a direct relationship involving addition, subtraction, and multiplication by constants.

Methods for Linear Equations:

  • Direct Methods:
    • Gauss Elimination Method
    • Gauss-Jordan Method
    • Pivoting
  • Iterative Method:
    • Gauss-Seidel Method
